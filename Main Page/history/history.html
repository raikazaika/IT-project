Where the chocolate has started

Chocolate has a rich and fascinating history that dates back to ancient Mesoamerican civilizations and they are the ones that found that the cocoa beans can be used for things like making chocolate.

It is known that chocolate has been eaten from around 1900 bc on lands that are currently mexican.

The discovery of chocolate in Europe

Chocolate was first discovered in Europe at the 17th century durning the Spanish invasion of the Aztecs. They were given a chocolate drink that had a bitter taste.

The people of Spain didnt really like the drink because it was so bitter so they decided to add sugar and other ingredients. The people really liked the sweet chocolate and it became popular all over Europe quickly.

The growing popularity of chocolate globally

While the chocolate production grew, people started trading it all over the world. Many cacao beans were planted over South America and West Africa to increase it's production.

During the Industrial Revolution new machines and technologies were made, so the production of chocolate was easier and it became more affordable. Because of this popular companies like Mars were created and they are still popular to this day.
