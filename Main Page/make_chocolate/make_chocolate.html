Homemade Dark Chocolate Recipe

Ingredients:

  • Cocoa butter: 1 cup (approximately 220 grams)
  • Cocoa powder: 1 cup (approximately 85 grams)
  • Honey or maple syrup: 1/4 cup (60 ml) (adjust to taste)
  • Vanilla extract: 1 teaspoon
  • Sea salt: 1/8 teaspoon

Optional Additions:

  • Nuts (e.g., almonds, hazelnuts)
  • Dried fruit (e.g., raisins, cranberries)
  • Spices (e.g., cinnamon, chili powder)
  • Orange zest

Equipment:

  • Double boiler or a heatproof bowl and a pot
  • Whisk
  • Silicone chocolate molds or a lined baking sheet
  • Spatula

Steps:

  1. Prepare the double boiler: Fill the bottom pot of the double boiler with about 2 inches of water. If you don't have a double boiler, place a heatproof bowl over a pot of simmering water, ensuring the bowl does not touch the water.
  2. Melt the cocoa butter: Place the cocoa butter in the top pot or the heatproof bowl. Heat gently over medium heat, stirring occasionally, until completely melted.
  3. Combine cocoa powder and sweetener: Once the cocoa butter is melted, reduce the heat to low. Gradually sift in the cocoa powder, whisking continuously to prevent lumps. Add honey or maple syrup slowly, whisking until fully incorporated. Taste and adjust sweetness if necessary.
  4. Add vanilla and salt: Stir in the vanilla extract and sea salt until well combined. Ensure the mixture is smooth and glossy.
  5. Incorporate optional add-ins (if using): If you want to add nuts, dried fruit, or spices, mix them into the chocolate now.
  6. Pour into molds: Carefully pour the chocolate mixture into silicone molds or spread it evenly on a lined.

Homemade Cacao Spread Recipe

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 cup raw cacao powder (approximately 50 grams)
  • 1/4 cup cocoa butter (approximately 55 grams)
  • 1/2 cup nut butter (e.g., almond, hazelnut, or peanut butter)
  • 1/4 cup maple syrup or honey (60 ml) (adjust to taste)
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/8 teaspoon sea salt

Optional additions:

  • 1-2 tablespoons coconut oil (for a smoother spread)
  • 1/2 teaspoon cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon instant coffee (for a mocha flavor)
  • Chopped nuts

Equipment:

  • Double boiler or a heatproof bowl and a pot
  • Whisk
  • Spatula
  • Jar or airtight container for storage

Steps:

  1. Prepare the double boiler:Fill the bottom pot of the double boiler with about 2 inches of water. If you don't have a double boiler, place a heatproof bowl over a pot of simmering water, ensuring the bowl does not touch the water.

  2. Melt the cocoa butter:Place the cocoa butter in the top pot or the heatproof bowl. Heat gently over medium heat, stirring occasionally, until completely melted.

  3. Combine cacao powder and nut butter: Once the cocoa butter is melted, reduce the heat to low. Gradually sift in the cacao powder, whisking continuously to prevent lumps. Add the nut butter and stir until fully incorporated and smooth.

  4. Add sweetener: Add maple syrup or honey slowly, whisking until fully incorporated. Taste and adjust sweetness if necessary.

  5. Add vanilla and salt:Stir in the vanilla extract and sea salt until well combined. Ensure the mixture is smooth and glossy.

  6. Incorporate optional add-ins (if using):If you want to add coconut oil, cinnamon, instant coffee, or chopped nuts, mix them into the spread now.

  7. Cool and store:Remove the mixture from heat and let it cool slightly. Pour the spread into a jar or airtight container.

  8. Refrigerate:Place the jar or container in the refrigerator for about 1-2 hours, or until the spread is set and firm but still spreadable.

  9. Enjoy:Once the cacao spread is set, it's ready to enjoy. Spread it on toast, use it as a dip for fruit, or incorporate it into your favorite desserts. Store the spread in the refrigerator to keep it fresh.
